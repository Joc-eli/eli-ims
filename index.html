⚠️ NC & Scrap Log

Log non-conforming material and scrapped items · Automatically deducts from on-hand quantity

⚠️ Non-Conformance (NC)

Material or product that is defective, damaged, non-functional, or doesn't meet specification (per 21 CFR 820). NC items are segregated and investigated — they may be reworked, returned to vendor, accepted as-is (with QA approval), or scrapped. The QA Manager is notified and a disposition is assigned. NC items stay in the system for traceability.

🗑️ Scrap

Material that has been permanently removed from inventory and safely disposed of. Scrapped items cannot re-enter the production system or be confused with conforming product. Scrap is a final disposition — the material is gone. Quantity is deducted from on-hand inventory immediately. Scrap typically results from an NC investigation, but can also occur during normal production (e.g. trim waste, damaged during assembly).

In short: NC = "something's wrong, let's investigate and decide what to do" → Scrap = "it's been decided — this material is permanently removed." Not all NCs become scrap (some get reworked or returned), but all scrap should be documented. Reference: SOP-10 §7.2.9
